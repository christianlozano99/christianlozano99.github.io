Academic Work

As a student, I had the privilege of enrolling in an exceptional Machine Learning course taught by my favorite Professor at UCF, Dr. Hollander. Under his expert guidance, I took on numerous engaging Machine Learning projects that not only deepened my practical understanding, but also provided me with invaluable insights into the underlying theoretical foundations of this field. It was a truly amazing learning experience that expanded my knowledge and ignited my passion for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence.

One of my favorite assignments during my academic journey was creating a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). This assignment was assigned to me about a year after the pandemic and involved building a model capable of classifying and predicting the specific type of lung damage depicted in X-ray images. The dataset provided for training the model consisted of X-ray images showcasing lungs affected by various conditions such as COVID-19, bacterial infections, and damage caused by stress smoking. In addition, a set of X-ray images featuring healthy, normal lungs was included. This assignment not only allowed me to apply my knowledge in CNNs but also provided a opportunity to contribute to the understanding and diagnosis of lung conditions through the power of machine learning.
